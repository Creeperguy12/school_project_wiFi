What is wiFi?

wiFi is a facility allowing devices to connect to the internet or communicate with others wirelessly in another area. It is WLAN (wireless local area network). It is essentially a wireless Ethernet.

Why is wiFi important?

We need wiFi because otherwise we would require a lot of Ethernet ports everywhere, and for a school, event or workplace this would be impractical, and this would also be expenisve. The solution is to use wiFi.

Who invented wiFi and when?

Invented in 3rd Industrial Revolution There was no specific inventor for wiFi. However, Dr John O'Sullivan and his colleagues Terence Percival, Graham Daniels, Diet Ostry and John Deane developed a key patent in wiFi. They are credited with the invention of wiFi.

Basic Info about wiFi

Wi-Fi compatible devices can connect to the Internet via a WLAN and a wireless access point. Such an access point (or hotspot) has a range of about 20 meters (66 feet) indoors and a greater range outdoors. Hotspot coverage can be as small as a single room with walls that block radio waves, or as large as many square kilometres achieved by using multiple overlapping access points.

The Technical Stuffs

But most importantly, Wi-Fi currently carries more than 60% of the world’s Internet traffic. Interestingly enough, this great achievement is basically done with radio waves, though it’s a little more complicated than your car stereo. Unlike the FM receiver in your car, Wi-Fi is essentially two radios communicating back and forth that use lower power and broadcast over a much shorter distance. These two radios let web users download data from the Internet as well as upload information — even just submitting addresses via your browser counts in this two-way communication.

wiFi, for the better or the worse?

Personally, I think wiFi is for the better. It has saved the world kilometers of ethernet cables and has helped schools, workplaces and events. It is only going to advance.
